Tax Alert:
Voluntary Tax Disclosure Programme
The Anti-Corruption And Economic Crimes Act provides that a person is guilty of an offence if he fraudulently or otherwise unlawfully fails to pay any taxes. The general penalty for this offence is a fine not exceeding KES 1 Million, or to imprisonment for a term not exceeding 10 years, or to both.
The government introduced the Voluntary Tax Disclosure Programme (VTDP) with effect from 1st January 2021. This programme introduced an avenue for taxpayers with previously undisclosed taxes to disclose and pay without imposition of punitive penalties and interest.
A taxpayer qualifies for the VTDP for tax liabilities that were accrued within the period 1 July 2015 to 1 July 2020.
The liabilities that can be disclosed under the VTDP ranges across various tax heads such as; Corporation Tax, Individual Income Tax, Pay As You Earn (PAYE), Value Added Tax (VAT), Withholding Tax (WHT), Excise Duty, Capital Gains Tax, Turnover Tax, Monthly Rental Income Tax
It is important to note that the VTDP does not apply to a taxpayer who:
  • is under audit, investigation or is a party to ongoing litigation in respect of the tax liability or any matter relating to the tax liability; or
  • has been notified of a pending audit or investigation by the Commissioner.
Successful applicants shall be granted a remission of the penalties and interest due as follows:
  • 100% waiver if the disclosure is done in the calendar year 2021;
  • 50% waiver if the disclosure is done in the calendar year 2022; and
  • 25% waiver if the disclosure is done in the calendar year 2023
The applications for VTDP are to be submitted online through the iTax portal. The applicant will receive approval/rejection notice via their registered email address.
